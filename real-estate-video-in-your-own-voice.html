Why your voice changes everything

Most agents fall into one of two camps. Either you're great on camera — in which case you're probably already filming. Or, like the large majority, you'd rather not be on camera at all, so your listings go out as silent slideshows with a music track. Buyers scroll right past those, and sellers can tell no one really made it for them.

There's a third way. A video that talks — "Welcome to 14 Crestline; let me show you the kitchen you're going to love" — in your own voice, over cinematic footage of the home, lands completely differently. It feels personal because it is. It's the difference between a stock reel and a tour the agent clearly made themselves. And you got there without ever stepping in front of a lens.

Clone once, narrate forever

The reason agents don't do voiceover today is the work: writing a script, finding a quiet room, re-recording the parts you flubbed, syncing it to the footage. Vistalia removes all of it. The marginal effort of adding voice to your hundredth listing is zero.
