June 21, 2026. Production batch for **Vistalia** launch week. These are the real
assets, not tests. Nine faceless cinematic **reveal** clips rendered on Kling 3.0 Turbo
(9:16, 1080p, Scottsdale desert-modern). Local copies in `marketing/higgsfield-ads/`,

## The offer (lock this on every end card)
- **First video free** — no credit card
- **$100 per video**, down to **$65 each** in a 10-pack
- **No subscription** — pay per listing
- Domain: **vistalia.ai** · Handle: **@vistalia** (claim across IG/TikTok/X/YouTube)
- End card: the **V** mark (`brand/vistalia-mark.svg` / `vistalia-avatar-1024.png`) on
  the `vistalia-endcard-bed` clip, then "vistalia.ai".

## Two hard lessons baked into this batch
1. **Never render these through Marketing Studio / UGC flow.** That flow forces an
   on-camera talking-head presenter every time (it overrode "faceless" on 4 EstateMotion
   ads last round). Faceless = Kling text-to-video only. Done.
2. **Kling reveals render silent by design.** The VO line + music bed + on-screen text +
   end card are added in post (CapCut/Premiere). Keep **one continuous music track across
   the hook→reveal flip** — the retained-audio trick that made the assembled ads land.

## The structure every ad uses
**Phone-real hook (0–1.5s, shot on a phone or text-on-screen, no AI) → flip/cut → cinematic
Vistalia reveal (the clip below) → offer card.** Hook in the first 1.5s. 15–25s total.
The hook must look native/un-produced; the *reveal* is where "looks expensive" earns the click.
