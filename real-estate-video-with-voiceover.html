Why "music-only" isn't enough anymore

Listings with video get dramatically more engagement than those without — but the bar has moved. When every agent can generate a music-over-photos reel in two minutes, those reels stop standing out. The video that holds attention now is the one that guides the viewer: a voice walking them through the kitchen, the light, the lot. Narration is what turns a pretty clip into a tour someone actually finishes.

The narration writes itself

The reason agents skip voiceover is the labor — scripting each property, recording cleanly, editing it to match the cuts. Vistalia automates the entire chain. It reads the listing, writes the narration, voices it, and syncs it to the footage. Your job is to review, not to produce. You can edit any line you want, but you start from a finished draft, not a blank page.

Pro voice or your own — your call

Want it polished and neutral? Choose a professional narrator. Want it personal, so sellers feel like you made it yourself? Narrate in your own cloned voice — recorded once, reused on every listing automatically. Both options ship a video that speaks, which already separates you from the silent reels flooding every feed.
