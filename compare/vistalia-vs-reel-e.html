The honest version: what each tool is really for

Reel-E is a volume tool. It does one thing very well — it takes the photos you already have, flies a smooth virtual camera through each one, cuts the moves to music, and hands you four formats in about two minutes. If you list a lot and you want a clean, cheap, "good enough" reel for every property plus a listing website thrown in, Reel-E earns its $44–$97 a month. We're not going to pretend otherwise.

Vistalia is a premium tool. We made a different bet. Instead of moving a camera across a flat photo, we use Google's Veo 3.1 to generate the motion — water actually moves, light actually shifts, the room feels filmed rather than scanned. And instead of leaving the video silent, we narrate it: in a professional voice, or in your voice, cloned once and reused on every listing forever.

The one difference that wins listings: your voice

Here's the gap nobody else is filling. Reel-E videos have no narration — they're music over moving photos. That's fine for a quick social post. But the videos that win a listing presentation, that make a seller feel like you personally walked them through the home, talk. They say "Welcome to 14 Crestline — let me show you the kitchen you're going to fall in love with," in the agent's own voice.

Vistalia clones your voice once. After that, every listing you ever make narrates itself in your voice automatically — no recording booth, no re-reading scripts. For agents who aren't comfortable on camera but want that personal touch, it's the whole product. Reel-E, by design, can't do this. See how your-own-voice narration works →

Pricing: subscription vs pay-per-listing

Reel-E is a monthly subscription. Even their entry plan asks you to commit annually to get the $44 rate, and you're paying whether you list that month or not. Vistalia charges per listing — $100 for one, dropping to $65 each in a 10-pack — and the first one is free with no card. If you list once a quarter, a subscription is money lit on fire; pay-per-listing fits how most agents actually work. If you list constantly, the 10-pack lands near Reel-E's effective per-video cost while giving you narrated, generative video instead of silent pans.

When Reel-E is the better choice

We'd rather you pick the right tool than churn. Choose Reel-E if: you push 10+ listings a month and need the absolute lowest cost per video; you want a bundled listing website and photo-editing/staging in the same subscription; or you genuinely prefer a fixed monthly bill. Choose Vistalia if: you want your videos narrated in your own voice; you want generative motion that looks filmed, not scanned; you'd rather pay per listing; or you just want to see the difference yourself before paying a cent.

Frequently asked questions

Does Reel-E have voiceover?
No — Reel-E videos are music-only, with no narration option anywhere on their platform. Vistalia narrates every video, either in a professional narrator's voice or in a clone of your own voice.
Is Vistalia a good Reel-E alternative?
It's the strongest alternative if you want your listing videos to actually speak — in your own voice — and to use true generative motion instead of camera pans on a photo. And because your first video is free, you can judge the output against Reel-E's yourself before paying. More on switching from Reel-E →
How much does each one cost?
Reel-E starts at $44/month (billed annually, ~3 listings/mo) up to $449/month. Vistalia is pay-per-video: $100 single, $375 for a 5-pack ($75 each), $650 for a 10-pack ($65 each), first video free, no subscription.
Are the videos MLS-compliant?
Vistalia is built for MLS compliance: you review every scene before publishing, so nothing misleading ships. Reel-E's approach never alters the photo, which is also MLS-safe. Both are designed not to misrepresent a property.
Can I really use my own voice?
Yes. You record a short sample once; Vistalia clones it and narrates every future listing in your voice automatically. You can also choose a professional narrator instead. See voiceover options →
